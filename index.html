Are Ghegs (Northern Albanians) more Illyrians than Tosks? (Southern Albanians)

Throughout history, the Ghegs of Northern Albania

have been considered by some historians

and anthropologists to be more closely

related to the ancient Illyrians than

the Tosks of Southern Albania. This

article explores the cultural, linguistic,

and historical differences between the Ghegs

and Tosks and the extent of their

connection to the Illyrians, an ancient

people who once inhabited the western Balkans.

Historical Context

The Illyrians were a group of tribes

that lived in the western Balkans

during ancient times. Many historians

believe that the Ghegs, with their

language and traditions, have preserved

more elements of the ancient Illyrian

culture compared to the Tosks. The

Tosks, while also descended from Illyrian

roots, have undergone more external

influences over time.

Linguistic Differences

One of the key factors in determining

the relation between Ghegs and Illyrians

lies in language. The Gheg dialect,

spoken in Northern Albania, is often

seen as more archaic and closer

to the language spoken by the Illyrians,

while the Tosk dialect has evolved

differently over time, influenced by

different cultural and linguistic groups.

Cultural Influence

Culture also plays a significant role.

The Ghegs have maintained many traditional

practices that can be traced back

to Illyrian customs. From the organization

of their families to their social

structure, these practices bear resemblance

to ancient Illyrian ways. On the

other hand, the Tosks were influenced

by the Byzantine Empire, Ottoman rule,

and other external factors, which altered

their customs over time.
